About Chocolate Truffles

Chocolate truffles are a luxurious treat made from a ganache center coated in cocoa powder, nuts, or chocolate. They originated in France in the late 19th century and were named after the black truffle fungus due to their resemblance. Today, they are enjoyed worldwide as a decadent dessert.

Interesting Facts

  • Chocolate truffles were originally created as a way to use up leftover ganache.
  • The world's largest truffle weighed over 2,000 pounds and was created in Italy.
  • The most expensive chocolate truffle sold for over $250 each and was decorated with gold leaf.

Nutritional Information

While chocolate truffles are delicious, they are also high in calories and sugar. Here's a typical nutritional breakdown for a single truffle:

  • Calories: 80
  • Total Fat: 6g
  • Saturated Fat: 4g
  • Cholesterol: 10mg
  • Sodium: 5mg
  • Total Carbohydrates: 7g
  • Sugars: 6g
